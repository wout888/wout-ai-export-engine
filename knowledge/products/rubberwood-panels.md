# Rubberwood Panels
## Product Summary
Rubberwood panels are one of our core product categories and have been supplied for many years to demanding international markets, particularly the United States.
Rubberwood is a plantation-grown hardwood originally cultivated for latex production. After its productive life, the timber is harvested and processed into a wide range of value-added wood products.
In our experience, rubberwood is particularly suitable for countertops, worktops, furniture components and many interior applications.

# Typical Construction
## Finger Joint Panels
Preferred construction for most applications.
Reasons:
- Better utilization of raw material
- Excellent stability
- Practical manufacturing solution
- Well suited to available rubberwood log sizes
Typical stave widths:
- 10 mm
- 15 mm
- 20 mm
- 25 mm
- 38 mm
- 40 mm
- 45 mm
- 50 mm
Our own production commonly uses approximately 38-50 mm stave widths.
## Edge Glued / Wide Plank
Possible, but considerably more difficult.
Reason:
Rubber trees generally produce relatively small logs, making large clear wide planks naturally more difficult to source consistently.
For many applications, finger-jointed construction is technically the better solution.

# Advantages
## Structural Performance
Rubberwood is generally an excellent construction material with stable and predictable characteristics.
## Consistency
Compared to some alternative species, rubberwood tends to have relatively consistent material properties.
Density variation is generally less extreme than can be encountered with acacia from different regions.
## Appearance
One of its strongest commercial advantages.
Light colour.
Clean appearance.
Often visually compared with birch.
Particularly popular in the United States.
Our own sales into the US market have historically been predominantly rubberwood rather than acacia.
---
# Limitations
## Outdoor Use
Not recommended for exterior applications.
Without proper protection and maintenance it is susceptible to deterioration and rot.
Indoor use is strongly preferred.
## Dark Paint Finishes
Rubberwood is naturally very light in colour.
When painted black or another dark colour, scratches can expose the underlying light wood, making damage visually obvious.
---
# Moisture Content
Critical quality parameter.
Typical target:
8% - 12%
Around 8% is preferred.
Above 12% should generally be avoided.
Rubberwood is relatively easy to dry correctly and moisture should not normally be a major issue in properly managed production.
---
# Grading
Grading must always be clearly defined with the customer.
"AB" quality has different meanings to different buyers and suppliers.
Never assume grading definitions are universal.
Alignment on grading standards before production is essential.
---
# Glue
Preferred adhesive system:
D4
Glue quality is an important part of long-term product performance.
Our organization also has practical experience with Koyo Bond adhesive products in Vietnam.
---
# Sanding
Typical specifications:
- Grit 180
- Grit 240
- Grit 320
Selection depends entirely on customer requirements.
180 and 240 are the most common.
---
# Warping
Warping is particularly important for thinner panels.
For thicker products (38 mm and 44 mm), properly manufactured rubberwood panels generally perform very well.
Correct moisture control remains essential.
---
# Packaging
Unfinished products should preferably be packed inside sealed plastic bags (shrink wrap) to reduce moisture exchange during transport and storage.
Packaging quality is an important quality-control point.
---
# Quality Inspection Checklist
When opening a container or inspecting production, immediate attention should be given to:
- Colour selection consistency
- Grain orientation
- Moisture content
- Finger joint quality
- Open glue joints
- Overall grading
- Surface sanding quality
- Panel flatness
- Warping
- Packaging quality
Dimensions are generally less critical than overall manufacturing quality and moisture control.

# FSC and PEFC
Customers may require FSC or PEFC certification.
Rubberwood itself originates from plantation-grown trees originally cultivated for latex production.
In practice, certification can be complicated because raw material often comes from many small individual farmers rather than one large plantation owner.
The absence of certification does not automatically imply poor forestry practices, but customer requirements should always be respected.
---
# Comparison with Acacia
Rubberwood:
- Lighter colour
- More birch-like appearance
- Better suited for many construction applications
- Generally more consistent material characteristics
Acacia:
- Slightly lower cost in many cases
- Different appearance
- Can contain water pockets
- Wide plank production can be more problematic due to internal moisture behaviour
- Often easier to source FSC-certified material from Vietnam
