# Acacia Panels

## Product Summary

Acacia is one of the most important solid wood products in our portfolio and is widely used for countertops, furniture and interior applications.

Compared with rubberwood, it offers a different visual appearance and different technical characteristics.

Selection between acacia and rubberwood should be based on the intended application rather than price alone.

---

# Typical Applications

- Kitchen countertops
- Kitchen islands
- Furniture
- Interior components
- Worktops
- DIY applications

Application requirements ultimately determine whether acacia or rubberwood is the better choice.

---

# General Characteristics

Typical characteristics include:

- Warm natural appearance
- Distinct grain pattern
- Hardwood construction
- Good value proposition
- Suitable for a wide variety of interior applications

---

# Comparison with Rubberwood

## Price

Acacia is often slightly less expensive than rubberwood, although market conditions can change.

## Appearance

Acacia has a much richer and more pronounced grain than rubberwood.

Rubberwood has a lighter appearance that many customers compare to birch.

Customer preference depends heavily on the target market.

## Material Characteristics

Rubberwood generally has more consistent density characteristics.

Acacia can vary significantly depending on its origin.

For example, material from northern Vietnam can be noticeably heavier than material from southern Vietnam.

---

# Water Pockets

One important practical characteristic of acacia is the presence of water pockets.

These can remain inside the timber and create localized moisture-related issues.

From practical experience, they can make wide plank production more challenging and increase the risk of movement or warping.

This is one reason why manufacturing quality and raw material selection are particularly important.

---

# Wide Plank vs Finger Joint

Wide plank acacia panels are possible.

However, due to internal moisture behaviour and water pockets, they can present additional challenges.

Proper material selection and drying are critical.

Finger-jointed construction often provides a more stable and predictable manufacturing solution.

---

# FSC

One practical advantage of Vietnam is that FSC-certified acacia is often relatively accessible.

For customers with FSC requirements, acacia can therefore be an attractive option.

Certification availability should always be confirmed for each project.

- Colour consistency
- Grading consistency
- Finger joint quality
- Surface quality
- Warping
- Packaging

As with all solid wood products, manufacturing discipline is often more important than marketing claims.

---

# Market Notes

Customer preferences differ significantly by market.

In the United States, our own experience has generally shown stronger demand for rubberwood compared with acacia.

Other regions may have different preferences based on appearance, pricing and application.
