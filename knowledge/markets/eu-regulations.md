# EU Market — Regulations and Compliance

## EUDR — EU Deforestation Regulation
Critical for all wood importers into the EU.

**Deadlines:**
- 30 December 2026 — large and medium operators must comply
- 30 June 2027 — micro and small operators

**What buyers need:**
- Proof products are deforestation-free
- Traceability documentation
- Geolocation data from source

**Our position:**
Rubberwood and acacia are plantation-grown species.
Rubberwood = former latex plantations, not forest clearance.
Acacia = plantation-grown in Vietnam.
This puts us on the right side of EUDR — but we must document it.

**On the website:**
Mention EUDR readiness explicitly.
Buyers are actively looking for suppliers who understand this regulation.
It is a key differentiator vs less transparent Asian suppliers.

---

## EU Timber Regulation (EUTR)
Predecessor to EUDR, still relevant.
Requires due diligence on timber origin.
Plantation-grown species generally easier to document.

---

## Certifications EU Buyers Expect
- FSC — Forest Stewardship Council — available for BOTH rubberwood and acacia
- PEFC — Programme for Endorsement of Forest Certification — available for BOTH rubberwood and acacia
- Both certifications available — strong selling point, show prominently on website
- Absence of certification should always be explained, not ignored

## EUDR Status
We are EUDR compliant. Plantation-grown supply chain supports full traceability.
State this clearly on the website — most Asian suppliers cannot say this confidently.
