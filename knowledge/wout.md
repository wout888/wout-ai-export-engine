# Wout – Working Philosophy and Decision Framework
## Purpose
This document does not describe factual knowledge.
It describes how Wout thinks, evaluates opportunities and makes business decisions.
Future AI systems should use this document to better understand his priorities, communication style and decision-making process.
---
# Core Principle
I use AI to scale my knowledge and experience, not to replace my judgment.
AI is a multiplier.
Human judgment remains essential.
---
# My Business Philosophy
I do not believe my value comes from websites, software or presentations.
My value comes from:
- Finding opportunities
- Visiting factories
- Understanding products
- Evaluating quality
- Understanding pricing
- Building relationships
- Creating trust
- Reducing friction between buyers and suppliers
Technology should amplify those strengths.
---
# What I Actually Sell
I do not sell wood.
I do not sell factories.
I sell confidence.
I sell experience.
I sell trust.
I reduce uncertainty for buyers and help trustworthy factories reach the right customers.
---
# Reputation
My reputation is my most valuable asset.
I only want to represent products and factories that I personally trust.
I prefer to lose business rather than damage my reputation.
---
# Commission Model
My preferred business model is commission-based representation.
I generally prefer introducing customers directly to factories while creating value through:
- product knowledge
- communication
- quality understanding
- negotiation
- practical experience
- relationship management
The objective is long-term cooperation rather than maximizing short-term margins.
---
# Execution Philosophy
Ideas are cheap.
Execution creates value.
Fast execution creates competitive advantage.
Testing ten ideas quickly is often better than endlessly discussing one perfect idea.
---
# AI Philosophy
Knowledge should live in structured Markdown files under version control.
The repository is the permanent memory.
AI providers are replaceable tools.
Vendor lock-in should be avoided wherever possible.
Knowledge first.
Tools second.
Models third.
---
# Practicality
I prefer practical solutions over technically impressive solutions.
Simple systems that work are better than elegant systems that never get deployed.
Low overhead matters.
Execution speed matters.
Commercial value matters.
---
# Continuous Improvement
I strongly value process improvement.
The objective is not fixing today's mistake.
The objective is making sure the same mistake never happens again.
This philosophy applies to factories, businesses and software.
---
# Decision Making
I rely heavily on experience and intuition built through years of international business.
Many decisions cannot be reduced to spreadsheets or checklists.
People matter.
Trust matters.
Character matters.
Long-term behaviour matters.
---
# Evaluating Factories
I pay attention to:
- process quality
- management quality
- production organization
- quality systems
- communication
- material control
- long-term thinking
- ability to continuously improve
Marketing presentations have little value.
Real operations matter.
---
# Evaluating People
Trust is built over time.
Small successful cooperation is preferable to large promises.
Experience with people cannot easily be replaced by AI.
Character is often more important than presentations.
---
# Risk Philosophy
Start small.
Validate.
Build trust.
Expand.
Avoid unnecessary risk.
Avoid betting everything on assumptions.
---
# Learning
Curiosity is a major driving force.
I enjoy understanding new technologies and finding practical ways to apply them.
Money is a result.
Building useful things is the motivation.
---
# AI Export Engine
The objective is not to build websites.
The objective is to build a reusable execution engine that transforms structured knowledge into professional commercial assets with minimal time and overhead.
The knowledge base is the real asset.
Everything else can be regenerated.
---
# Communication Style
Prefer:
- concise
- structured
- practical
- honest
- direct
Avoid:
- unnecessary brainstorming
- long theoretical discussions
- overcomplicated solutions
- technology for its own sake
Stay focused on the current deliverable.
Output is more valuable than discussion.
If focus is lost, return to the current objective immediately.
